Tourism and Economic Performance in Malaysia

Tourism is one of Malaysia’s strongest economic pillars, driving growth, creating jobs, and supporting local industries. Each year, millions of visitors contribute to the nation’s GDP through spending on travel, hospitality, and services.

When the COVID-19 pandemic struck, international arrivals plummeted — revealing just how vital tourism is to Malaysia’s economy. This visualization explores the close relationship between tourism trends and GDP performance, showing how both declined during the pandemic and how recovery is now taking shape.

Points of Entry in Malaysia (2020-2023)

The sharp decline in foreign arrivals in 2020 and 2021 is a direct result of the COVID-19 pandemic. On March 18, 2020, Malaysia implemented a nationwide Movement Control Order (MCO) that effectively closed its international borders to tourists. These national measures, combined with the global shutdown of air travel, kept arrival numbers at historic lows until the gradual reopening of borders began in 2022.

Who Visits Malaysia the Most?

The chart shows that Singapore, Indonesia, and Thailand are the top three source countries of tourist arrivals to Malaysia, both within Asia and worldwide. This reflects Malaysia’s strong regional and global dependence on ASEAN neighbours. Most arrivals from Singapore and Thailand enter by land, while Indonesia contributes through a mix of air and sea travel. Their dominant share highlights the importance of regional connectivity, cross-border tourism, and close cultural ties in driving Malaysia’s overall inbound tourism performance.

Gender Breakdown of Tourists

This interactive donut chart visualizes the gender distribution of tourists visiting Malaysia from 2020 to 2023. The blue segment represents female tourists, while the red segment represents male tourists.

2020: The gender distribution was relatively balanced, with male tourists slightly outnumbering females.
2021: A significant drop in female tourist arrivals is observed, likely due to COVID-19 travel restrictions — resulting in a male-dominated tourist flow.
2022–2023: Female arrivals increased again, restoring a more balanced gender composition similar to pre-pandemic levels.

The Year slider allows users to explore yearly changes, while the Country filter enables focused comparisons of tourist composition by country of origin.

Tourism Sector Contribution to GDP

The area chart illustrates the contribution of various economic sectors to Malaysia's Real GDP from 2020 to 2023. Notably, the Transportation and Storage sector consistently holds the largest share, reflecting its critical role in supporting tourism and trade. The Tourism Agencies sector also shows significant contributions, highlighting the importance of tourism-related services in the economy. Other sectors such as Food and Beverage, Private Health Services, and Private Education Services further emphasize the diverse economic activities linked to tourism.
